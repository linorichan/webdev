Biology is the study of living things. A large variety of living things exist on the land, in the water and in the air.
Living things include both plants and animals. Thus, biology deals with the study of all plants and animals that lived or have ever lived on the earth.


Cell biology is the study of cells.
Cells were first observed in the 17th century, when the compound microscope was invented.
Population biology deals with groups or populations of organisms that inhabit a given area or region.
A field of biology may be especially concerned with the investigation of one kind of living thing.
